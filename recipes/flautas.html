Flautas

Description

Flautas are a take on the infamous tacos from Mexican cuisine. These tacos are rolled tightly and fried, giving them the shape of "flutes," hence the name flautas.

They can be filled with any form of protein or mashed potatoes. But, this recipe will include shredded chicken as the filling of choice.

Ingredients

  • Shredded, cooked chicken
  • 1 cup of shredded cheddar
  • 1 cup of queso blanco
  • 1/2 tsp. of kosher salt
  • 2 tsp. of hot sauce (optional)
  • 10 7" flour tortillas
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable, canola, or other oil
  • For serving: pico de gallo, sour cream, and guacamole

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine chicke, cheeses, salt, and hot sauce (if using).
  2. Lay 1 tortilla down on a flat surface. Spread 1/4 cup of the chicken mixture onto the bottom third of the tortilla. Roll up tightly and place seam side down on a plate. Repeat with the remaining tortillas and filling.
  3. In a skillet, heat oil over medium-high heat until the temperature reaches between 350-375 degrees F. (A good tip is to hear a sizzle when the food hits the oil.)
  4. Add 3 of the flautas to the oil, seam side down. Cook, turning every 30 to 60 seconds, until golden all over, about 2 to 3 minutes. Transfer the flautas to a large paper towel-lined plate. Repeat with remaining flautas. Serve immediately with pico de gallo, sour cream, and guacamole.
